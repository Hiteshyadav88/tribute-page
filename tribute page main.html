Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an eminent Indian scientist, aerospace engineer, and the 11th President of India. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he hailed from a modest background. Kalam's brilliance in aeronautical engineering led him to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He played a pivotal role in India's satellite launch vehicle and nuclear weapons programs.

Known as the "People's President," Kalam was loved for his warm, approachable nature and was a source of inspiration for millions, particularly the youth. He authored several books, including the widely acclaimed "Wings of Fire," an autobiography chronicling his remarkable journey.

His vision for a developed India by 2020, coupled with his emphasis on education and innovation, left an indelible mark on the nation. Dr. Kalam's sudden passing in 2015 while delivering a lecture in Shillong was met with deep sorrow, but his legacy lives on through his contributions to science and his enduring impact on generations of Indians.
